Export Compliance Policy

MICA Supply LTD. is committed to strict adherence to all applicable export control laws and regulations, including those administered by the Government of Canada and the United States Department of Commerce.

⚠️ Regulatory Notice: The hardware, electronic components, and AI technology sourced and distributed by MICA Supply LTD. are subject to the U.S. Export Administration Regulations (EAR) enforced by the Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS).

Bureau of Industry and Security (BIS) Compliance

As a global hardware broker, we enforce strict supply chain integrity protocols. We do not engage in transactions that violate BIS controls. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Prohibiting the export, re-export, or transfer of restricted advanced computing chips and semiconductor manufacturing equipment to embargoed destinations or sanctioned entities.
  • Screening all downstream clients against the BIS Entity List, Denied Persons List, and Unverified List prior to quote fulfillment.

End-User Statement (EUS) Requirement

To mitigate diversion risks and comply with extraterritorial trade laws, MICA Supply LTD. requires a completed and signed End-User Statement (EUS) from the ultimate consignee for high-performance components.

Before any formal quote is finalized or purchase order is accepted, the downstream buyer must explicitly declare:

  • End-User Identity: The exact corporate entity and physical location where the hardware will be installed.
  • End-Use Application: The specific commercial or enterprise application of the components (e.g., enterprise data centers, cloud rendering).
  • Military End-Use (MEU) Prohibition: Certification that the hardware will not be used, directly or indirectly, for military intelligence, weapons development, or by any military end-user.

Failure to provide accurate EUS documentation, or the detection of red flags regarding potential diversion, will result in the immediate cancellation of the RFQ process.
